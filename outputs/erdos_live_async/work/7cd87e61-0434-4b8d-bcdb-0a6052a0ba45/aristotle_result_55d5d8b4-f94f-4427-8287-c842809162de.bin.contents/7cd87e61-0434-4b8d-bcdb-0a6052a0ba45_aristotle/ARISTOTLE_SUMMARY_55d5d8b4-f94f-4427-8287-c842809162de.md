# Summary of changes
## Summary

### Counterexample for the most fragile variant (fully proved ✅)

The "most fragile variant" of Erdős Problem 123 is obtained by **dropping the pairwise coprimality condition**. I proved this variant is **false** with the concrete counterexample `a = 2, b = 4, c = 8`:

- **`powTripleSet_248_subset_pow2`**: Every element of `PowTripleSet 2 4 8` is a power of 2 (since `2^i · 4^j · 8^k = 2^(i+2j+3k)`).
- **`pow2_division_chain`**: Any two distinct powers of 2 satisfy a divisibility relation (they are totally ordered).
- **`antichain_pow2_card_le_one`**: A division antichain drawn from powers of 2 has at most one element.
- **`not_eventually_pow2`**: Not every sufficiently large natural number is a power of 2.
- **`fragile_variant_counterexample`** (main result): `¬ IsDComplete (PowTripleSet 2 4 8)` — the theorem fails without coprimality, even though all three bases exceed 1.

All proofs use only standard axioms (`propext`, `Classical.choice`, `Quot.sound`).

### Main theorem (remains `sorry`)

The theorem `erdos_123_d_complete_sequences` — asserting that `PowTripleSet a b c` is d-complete for pairwise coprime `a, b, c > 1` — is Erdős Problem 123, a deep result in combinatorial number theory. Its proof requires extensive infrastructure (Chinese Remainder Theorem for digit representations, greedy construction of antichain decompositions, density arguments) that would constitute a major formalization effort well beyond a single session. The `sorry` remains with an updated docstring explaining this.

### Fixed infrastructure

The `lakefile.toml` was updated to the Lake v5 format (flat keys instead of `[package]` section) and `Main` was added as a build target.
